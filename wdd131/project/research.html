Your comprehensive roadmap to discovering and documenting your family's past

Getting Started: Where to Begin

Starting your family history research can feel overwhelming, but following these foundational steps will set you on the right path:

1

Start with What You Know

Begin by documenting information about yourself and your immediate family. Record names, birth dates, marriage dates, and any other significant events. This becomes your foundation.

2

Talk to Living Relatives

Interview parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, and cousins. Record their memories, stories, and any information they have about previous generations. Use our story submission form to preserve these narratives.

3

Gather Family Documents

Collect birth certificates, marriage licenses, death certificates, immigration records, military service documents, old letters, photographs, and family Bibles. These primary sources are invaluable.

4

Organize Your Findings

Create a systematic way to store and categorize your research. Use digital tools, folders, or databases to keep everything accessible and organized by family line or generation.

Conducting Family Interviews

Oral history interviews are one of the most valuable tools in family history research. Here are tips for conducting effective interviews:

Preparation

  • Prepare questions in advance but remain flexible
  • Choose a quiet, comfortable setting
  • Bring recording equipment (with permission)
  • Have photos or documents to prompt memories

During the Interview

  • Start with easy, non-threatening questions
  • Listen more than you talk
  • Ask open-ended questions
  • Be patient and allow time for reflection
  • Follow interesting tangents

After the Interview

  • Transcribe important details while fresh
  • Organize and label recordings properly
  • Send a thank you note
  • Share findings with other family members

Sample Interview Questions

  • Where and when were you born?
  • What are your earliest memories?
  • What was your childhood home like?
  • Tell me about your parents and siblings.
  • What games did you play as a child?
  • What do you remember about your grandparents?
  • What family stories were passed down to you?
  • Where did our family originally come from?
  • Do you remember any family traditions?
  • What languages were spoken in your home?
  • Tell me about your education.
  • What was your first job?
  • How did you meet your spouse?
  • What major historical events do you remember?
  • What are you most proud of in your life?
